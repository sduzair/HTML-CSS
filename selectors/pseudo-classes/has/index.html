Unit `px` stands for "pixels" and is an absolute unit of measurement. One pixel is equal to one dot on a screen, and the size of a pixel is determined by the screen's resolution. `px` is commonly used for setting precise measurements, such as the width and height of an element.

Point 2

Unit `px` stands for "em-height" and is a relative unit of measurement. One `em` is equal to the font-size of the element it is applied to. For example, if the font-size of a paragraph is `16px`, then `1em` is equal to `16px`. `em` is commonly used for setting measurements that are relative to the font-size of an element, such as the padding or margin around text.
